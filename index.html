Uncle Roger-style fried rice

Uncle Roger-style fried rice, inspired by the comedic persona of Malaysian stand-up comedian Nigel Ng, emphasizes a few key principles and techniques, often delivered with his signature blend of humor and strong opinions ("Haiyaa!" "Fuiyoh!").

Ingredients

  • Day-Old Cooked Rice
  • Oil with a High Smoke Point
  • Garlic
  • Shallots
  • Eggs
  • Soy Sauce
  • MSG
  • Spring Onions

Instructions

  1. Prep your ingredients
    • Dice your garlic, shallots, ginger (if using), and vegetables.
    • Cook and chop any protein if needed. Have your day-old rice ready and broken up.
    • Prepare your soy sauce mixture if you're combining them beforehand.
  2. Heat the wok
    • Get your wok or pan ripping hot over high heat. Add your oil. It should shimmer almost immediately.
  3. Cook aromatics
    • Add the garlic and shallots (and ginger, if using) and stir-fry quickly until fragrant and lightly golden
  4. Cook the egg
    • Push the aromatics to the side and crack your eggs into the wok.
    • Scramble them lightly until just cooked but still slightly wet.
    • Then, mix them in with the aromatics.
  5. Add the rice
    • Add the cold, day-old rice to the wok.
    • Break it up with your spatula and toss it to coat it in the oil and mix it with the eggs and aromatics.
    • Fry the rice for a few minutes, ensuring it heats through and starts to dry out slightly.
  6. Add protein and vegetables (if using)
    • If using cooked protein and other vegetables, add them now and stir-fry until heated through.
  7. Season
    • Add your soy sauce (light and dark, and sweet if using), white pepper, and MSG (if using).
    • Toss everything thoroughly to distribute the seasoning evenly.
  8. Add spring onions
    • Add the chopped spring onion (the white parts earlier for more flavor, the green parts towards the very end for freshness and color).
    • Toss briefly.
  9. Finish
    • Drizzle with sesame oil (if using) and give it a final toss.
